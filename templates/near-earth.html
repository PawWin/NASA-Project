Objects Near Earth

Potentially Hazardous Asteroids (PHAs) are a group of asteroids that may pose some risk to Earth due to their orbits and proximity to our planet. The main criteria that define potentially hazardous asteroids include:

Size: Potentially hazardous asteroids typically have a diameter greater than 140 meters. Their greater mass may cause greater damage in the event of a collision with Earth.

Orbit: These asteroids have orbits that exceed certain values, which means they come close enough to Earth's orbit to pose a potential collision hazard.
