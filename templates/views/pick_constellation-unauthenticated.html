Constellations

Constellations are star patterns recognized by ancient civilizations like the Babylonians and Greeks. These celestial designs, often depicting mythological figures, served as navigational guides and cultural inspirations throughout history. For example, Orion, a prominent winter constellation, symbolizes a Greek hunter and showcases the dual nature of constellations: cultural symbols that also aid modern astronomy. While stars within constellations vary in distance, their enduring patterns bridge ancient legends with today's understanding of the cosmos.
